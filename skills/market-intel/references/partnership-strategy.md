# Partnership Strategy

> "Method over hacks. Evidence over assumption."

## TL;DR

Partner identification, qualification/scoring, co-marketing program design, and referral-system mechanics. Outputs follow MetodologIA brand standards and the Alfa evidence-tag set. [EXPLICIT]

**In scope:** partner shortlist + fit scores, program structure, referral incentive design, success metrics, kill criteria.
**Anti-scope (do NOT produce here):** legal contract drafting, pricing/commission dollar figures, signed-deal forecasting, CRM data entry. Redirect those out. [EXPLICIT]

## Partner taxonomy (pick the type before scoring — mechanics differ)

| Type | What they trade | Primary motion | Watch-out |
|------|-----------------|----------------|-----------|
| Referral / affiliate | Warm intros for a reward | Volume, low-touch | Quality decays as reward rises [INFERENCE] |
| Co-marketing | Shared audience + content | Joint webinars, co-branded assets | Audience overlap kills lift [INFERENCE] |
| Technology / integration | Complementary product surface | API/embed, joint roadmap | Roadmap drift breaks the integration [ASSUMPTION] |
| Reseller / channel | Resale rights + margin | Their sales team sells yours | Channel conflict with direct sales [INFERENCE] |
| Strategic / OEM | Deep co-build or white-label | Exec-sponsored, multi-quarter | Lock-in and dependency risk [ASSUMPTION] |

## Procedure

### Step 1: Discover
- Gather the goal (which type above, what outcome) and the current ICP/audience. [EXPLICIT]
- Read existing partner lists, prior co-marketing results, CRM/referral logs if present. [EXPLICIT]
- Identify the gap the partner is meant to close: reach, credibility, product surface, or distribution. [INFERENCE]

### Step 2: Analyze — qualify with the fit score (do not skip to outreach)
Score each candidate 1–5 on five axes; weight, sum, rank. Default weights below; adjust and state why. [ASSUMPTION]

| Axis | Question | Weight |
|------|----------|:------:|
| Audience overlap | Same ICP, *non-overlapping* customers? | 0.30 |
| Reach | Net-new audience size they expose | 0.20 |
| Strategic fit | Complementary, not competing offer | 0.20 |
| Effort to activate | Time-to-first-joint-output | 0.15 |
| Reputation | Brand safety, track record | 0.15 |

- Score = Σ(axis × weight). Pursue ≥ 3.5; park 2.5–3.5; drop < 2.5. [ASSUMPTION]
- "Audience overlap" is high *only* when the ICP matches but customers don't — same ICP + same customers means cannibalization, not partnership. [INFERENCE]
- Evaluate top candidates against Constitution principles XIII (Think First) and XIV (Simple First): prefer the partner needing the least new machinery to launch. [EXPLICIT]

### Step 3: Execute — design the program
Choose the motion from the taxonomy, then specify, in order:
1. **Offer** — what each side gives and gets (asset, audience, reward), symmetric or stated-asymmetric. [EXPLICIT]
2. **Mechanic** — referral: tracking method (code/link/portal) + attribution window + reward trigger (qualified vs closed). Co-marketing: one concrete first artifact (webinar, guide, swap). [EXPLICIT]
3. **Reward shape** — describe structure (flat, tiered, revenue-share) WITHOUT dollar figures; pricing is anti-scope. [EXPLICIT]
4. **Owner + cadence** — single accountable owner each side, review rhythm. [EXPLICIT]
5. Apply evidence tags to every claim; render any HTML via the brand template (references/brand/). [EXPLICIT]

### Step 4: Validate
- Confirm fit scores are evidence-backed, not asserted. [EXPLICIT]
- Confirm metrics + kill criteria are defined before launch (below). [EXPLICIT]
- Check evidence-tag coverage and Constitution compliance. [EXPLICIT]

## Success metrics & kill criteria

| Metric | Healthy signal | Kill trigger [ASSUMPTION] |
|--------|----------------|---------------------------|
| Qualified referrals / mo | Rising, quality holds | 2 cycles below floor → pause |
| Referral → qualified rate | Stable or up | Halves vs baseline → audit fraud/fit |
| Co-marketing lift | Net-new reach > effort cost | Two events, no net-new audience → stop |
| Time-to-first-output | Days, not quarters | No joint output in agreed window → deprioritize |

Define the floor and window *with the partner* at launch; an undefined kill criterion is an open-ended cost. [INFERENCE]

## Worked example

Goal: extend reach for a B2B methodology product. Candidate: an agile-coaching newsletter (8k subscribers, same ICP, no product overlap).
Score: overlap 5×.30 + reach 4×.20 + fit 5×.20 + effort 4×.15 + reputation 4×.15 = **4.40 → pursue**. [INFERENCE]
Program: co-marketing. First artifact = co-branded guide + one joint webinar. Reward = audience swap (symmetric, no cash). Owner each side, monthly review. Kill: two events with no net-new signups → stop. [EXPLICIT]

## Failure modes

| Failure mode | Symptom | Counter |
|--------------|---------|---------|
| Logo-collecting | Many "partners", zero joint output | Require a first artifact to count as active [INFERENCE] |
| Reward-driven low quality | Referral volume up, qualified rate down | Trigger reward on *qualified*, not raw, leads [INFERENCE] |
| Channel conflict | Partner and direct sales chase same account | Rules of engagement before launch [ASSUMPTION] |
| Overlap blindness | Co-marketing to the same shared list | Require net-new-audience estimate pre-launch [INFERENCE] |
| Single point of failure | One partner = majority of pipeline | Cap dependency; diversify partner mix [ASSUMPTION] |

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It's Bad | Do This Instead |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Acting without understanding | Wastes effort on wrong partner | Think First (XIII) — qualify before outreach |
| Over-engineering | Tiered portal before first referral | Simple First (XIV) — manual link, then automate |
| Missing evidence tags | Claims without basis | Tag every assertion |
| Outreach before scoring | Chases low-fit partners | Run the fit score first |

## Edge Cases

| Scenario | Handling |
|----------|----------|
| Empty or minimal input | Request ICP + goal before proceeding |
| Conflicting requirements | Flag conflicts explicitly, propose resolution |
| Out-of-scope request (pricing, contract) | Redirect to legal/commercial; do not draft here |
| No candidate scores ≥ 2.5 | Report "no viable partner"; do not force a shortlist |
| Tie in fit scores | Break by lowest effort-to-activate (fastest learning) [INFERENCE] |
