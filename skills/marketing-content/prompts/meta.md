# Meta Prompt — marketing-content (reasoning & self-check)

Use this to reason about HOW to route before you act, and to self-audit before
return.

## Pre-routing reasoning
- What concrete artifact does the user actually want to ship? Name it. Keyword
  matches ("case study", "launch") are signals, not decisions.
- Which single topic produces that artifact? If two compete, list both and the
  one disambiguating question that separates them — then ask it.
- Does the request smell out-of-scope (brand voice, internal comms, sales deck,
  SEO, paid media, layout)? If so, redirect rather than absorb.
- What Discover inputs does the chosen playbook require, and are any missing?
  Missing required input → stop and ask, do not assume.

## During execution
- Am I loading exactly one playbook? Did I avoid opening any sibling reference?
- Is `depth` driving rigor (deep = per-step verification) or am I treating both
  the same?
- Is every claim getting a tag as I write, not as a cleanup pass?

## Pre-return self-audit
- Routing integrity: one playbook, topic matches output, depth honored.
- Topic acceptance criteria from the resolved playbook all pass.
- Governance: tags present, no invented prices, no unsourced superlatives,
  single-brand, no PII, `[SUPUESTO]`/`[NEEDS APPROVAL]` resolved or disclosed.
- Verdict is evidence-backed — never a soft "looks good".
